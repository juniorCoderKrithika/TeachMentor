Visual Learners

Traits of Visual Learners

Visual learners understand best when they see information. They may not understand spoken instructions. Visual students like diagrams, flowcharts, and graphs, and they find handouts helpful, where they can write down what they hear or see as it is being described. Visual learners notice small details, so they will remember faces instead of names, and they tend to like balance. They are often quiet students who like to read, draw, and do craft. They will watch a situation before getting involved. Visual learners express themselves through creativity.

Learning Strengths of Visual Learners

  • Can remember 75% of what they see or read
  • Have a good sense of direction because they can read maps
  • Tend to be neat due to their love of balance
  • Do well in class tests because they remember where the information is and can see it written down
  • May like color
  • Prefer looking at a person who is speaking to help them focus

Learning Strategies for Visual Learners

  • Write down the information people tell you and try to work on your own in a quiet place
  • Look at headings and pictures before reading whole sentences
  • Highlight important words in color
  • Write down goals of projects
  • Sit in a place where you can easily see the teacher
  • Write your own notes and use charts, maps, and graphs where possible
  • Use hands-on and practical tasks such as building models or doing case-studies
  • Connect new information with concepts you already know
  • Brainstorm and use diagrams and mind maps to show how everything fits together

Teaching Strategies for Visual Learners

  • Use lots of different technology to present information such as PowerPoint, videos, and photos
  • Organize information well with headings and pictures
  • Let the student be creative and use their imagination
  • After reading information, ask students to close their eyes so they can picture what they have just learned
  • When teaching, explain to students the reason they need to learn the information and how it will affect them
  • Always encourage discussion
  • Use handouts
  • Make sure there are no distractions
